Use the table given on the risk matrix to score your hazard or activity for the probability (‘L' ) likelihood harm will occur and the severity ('S') of the outcome.

Risk Score = S x L Risk Level
1-3 Low
4-6 Medium
8-10 High
10-16 Very High

Plot the scores on the matrix or multiply them together (LxS) to obtain a risk score – then use the colour of that score to determine the Risk Level, Low to Very High. Activities that are High or Very High must not start (or will need to be suspended), without appropriate controls in place to reduce the risk to an acceptable level. Wherever it is a possibility that a serious injury may occur risk must be lowered by putting in extra control measures.
